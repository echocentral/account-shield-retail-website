Get a flavor of Account Shield in action

A new user logs in to your site

When Account Shield is added to a website, it starts off with no knowledge of your existing users. Each time it is notified of a login, it can prompt the user to complete a short security setup.

As with all these screens, you can let Account Shield take care of them for you, or create your own using our templates and extensive documentation.

A new user provides their details

If you configure Account Shield with the One-Time-Password capability, your users will be asked to register their email and/or mobile number. Or, if you have this already, you can pass it through and avoid this collection step.

This is the only personal data held by Account Shield, and is securely encrypted and stored. This data is never associated with a name or address.

Users can review their account activity

Within your application, you can let your users see a history of their activity within their account. Account Shield automatically keeps a history of all logins and the pages visited during each session.

Providing this record to your users gives a strong indication that you take the security of their account seriously.

Users can review and manage their devices

At the heart of Account Shield is its ability to recognise and differentiate between devices. This effectively provides a multi-factor authentication capability, which can detect and prevent access attempts from unauthorised devices.

Within your application, you can let your users see a history of their devices - where and when they were used. Account Shield lets users de-authorise devices, providing strong protection if they should lose their smartphone, for example.

A login attempt with an unrecognised device is challenged

If Account Shield detects a login attempt made using an unknown device, it is able to notify the user sending them a one-time pass code. This is then used to verify that device, and allow access.

Hackers and fraudsters without the code, will not be able to gain access to the account.
